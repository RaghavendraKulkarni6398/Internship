\section{Examples 2}
\subsection{Question 13}

Prove(by shewing that the area of the triangle formed by them  is zero) that the following sets of three points are in a straight line $(a,b+c), (b,c+a)$ and $(c,a+b)$
\end{enumerate}
\subsection{Solution}
Rank of matrix method: 
\\If rank of matrix is not full matrix after row reduction, then points are said to be collinear
\begin{align}
\myvec{\myvec{\vec{A}-\vec{B}&\vec{A}-\vec{C}}^\top} \label{eq1}
\end{align}
Let $\vec{A}=\myvec{a\\b+c}, \vec{B}=\myvec{b\\c+a}, \vec{C}=\myvec{c\\a+b}$
\begin{align}
\vec{A}-\vec{B}&=\myvec{a\\b+c}-\myvec{b\\c+a}\\
&=\myvec{a-b\\b-a}\\
\vec{A}-\vec{C}&=\myvec{a\\b+c}-\myvec{c\\a+b}\\
&=\myvec{a-c\\c-a}
\end{align}
From \eqref{eq1}
\\Let 
\begin{align}
\vec{M}&=\myvec{a-b & a-c\\b-a & c-a}\\
\vec{M}&=\myvec{a-b & b-a\\a-c & c-a}
\end{align}
Row Reduction on
$\vec{M}$ \\$R_2 \longleftrightarrow R_2\times\myvec{a-b} - R_1\times\myvec{a-c}$ \nonumber
\begin{align}
\\\vec{M} & =\myvec{a-b & b-a\\0 & 0}\\
\end{align}
Since the rank of matrix $\vec{M}$ is 1, which is not full rank, 
\\therefore the given points are collinear.
